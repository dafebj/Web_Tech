The Roaring Twenties

The 1920s, also known as the "Roaring Twenties," was a time of cultural dynamism, technological advancements, and social change. It was an era of jazz music, flappers, and prohibition in the United States. The decade also saw the rise of cinema, radio, and modern consumerism.

Major Developments:

  • The rise of jazz and the Harlem Renaissance
  • The first radio broadcast
  • Advances in cinema, including the first "talkies"
